## What makes this a FORWARD test (not a backtest)

1. **Frozen configs** -- the candidate params are exactly as selected on 2020 (VAL/OOS Jul-Dec 2020). **No re-fit
   on 2021.** 2021 is pure out-of-selection-year forward. UNSEEN (2025-26) stays SEALED.
2. **Survivorship-clean point-in-time universe** -- membership is **derived from the data** (each asset's listing
   date = its first chimera bar), NOT from the 2026-curated u50/u100 yaml. Of 104 available symbols, **44 admitted**
   (listed by 2021) and **60 EXCLUDED** as post-2021 listings (SUI/TAO/WLD/ENA/APT/ARB/...). Each asset **enters the
   book at its own listing+warmup+liquidity date** -- the real asset-expansion dynamic.
3. **EXPAND vs CORE** -- the primary book is **active-roster EW** (EW across assets *active* at each bar, so the
   roster grows as coins list = realistic expansion); **u10-CORE** (10 majors, fixed roster) is the no-expansion
   baseline. expand-vs-core = the asset-expansion finding.
4. **Realistic maker fills** charged per flip; the MakerCostModel `p_fill 0.25-0.50` caveat => live equity ~50-75%
   of these numbers (flagged, not ignored).

## The three findings (each gap ironed)

### 1. The de-risked-beta CLASS generalizes; the within-class config ORDER does NOT
- **0/21 candidates beat buy-hold** (best KELT 158% vs core buy-hold 466%) -- the 2020 verdict (long-only
  trend/breakout/etc = under-participating de-risked beta) **holds out-of-selection-year on 2021.**
- **Rank-transfer: Spearman(2020 OOS net, 2021 fwd net) = 0.11 (core) / 0.09 (expand) ~ 0.** The 2020 config
  *ranking* carries **no information** about 2021 ranking. Poster child: **ADX(14,20)@4h was the 2020 BEST
  deployable (36.7%) and is the 2021 WORST (-18.4%)** -- a trend-strength filter that whipsawed in 2021's regime.
  KELT/MACD/PSAR (mid-pack on 2020) lead 2021. **Selection at the config level is regime-transient noise** -- do
  not deploy "the 2020 #1"; this is the same rank-instability the MA/TI work found, now confirmed across a year.

### 2. Drawdown-preservation is what the configs actually buy (and it generalizes cleanly)
- In the **May-2021 crash**, core buy-hold did **-58.9%**; **21/21 candidates LOST LESS** (VOLIMB -0.4%, OBV -1.3%,
  MFI -1.9%, VIDYA -2.2%, ... all configs roughly flat through the crash). The trend/breakout leaders (KELT/MACD)
  still compounded +120-158% on the year *while* sidestepping the crash.
- **This -- not bull-net -- is the value of the 2020-selected long-only configs:** they trade away bull
  participation for crash capital-preservation. That trade-off is robust and generalizes (it is the de-risked-beta
  thesis doing exactly what it says).

### 3. Asset expansion is not a free lunch
- Expansion **raises the beta ceiling** (expand buy-hold 678% vs core 466% -- the broad 2021 alt basket pumped).
- But strategies **capture it worse on the broad universe**: **EXPAND > CORE at only 4/21** candidates. The
  exceptions are the **volume-family signals** (OBV 2.2->18.6, VOLIMB 1.0->7.3, MFI 0.7->3.1) -- order-flow signals
  genuinely benefit from breadth. Trend/MA/momentum did *better* on the clean majors core (the broad universe adds
  whipsaw/noise from higher-vol, listing-staggered names that the trend filters fight). **Expansion helps specific
  (volume) signals, dilutes the rest.**

## Caveats (honest)
- **Residual survivorship:** coins that traded in 2021 but delisted before 2026 (LUNA/FTT/...) were never collected

  numbers, which are cleaner. Not fixable from the data we have.
