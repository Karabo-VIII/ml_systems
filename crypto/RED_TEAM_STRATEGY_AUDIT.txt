       Gate 1: win_rate >= 50% on >= 5/10 assets (cross-asset robustness)

       Ranked by avg_alpha. Reasonable anti-overfit framework.

MINOR CONCERNS (do not invalidate results):

  [NOTE] 50% window overlap (WINDOW_DAYS=30, STEP_DAYS=15)
         Creates correlated consecutive windows. If you get 6 windows,
         effective independent samples are ~3-4. The win_rate statistic
         (e.g., 4/6 beat market) has less statistical power than it appears.
         This does NOT bias Sharpe or alpha estimates -- it just means the
         confidence intervals are wider than they look.

  [NOTE] Sparse predictions after causal fix
         With stride=96 non-overlapping windows, each prediction covers 96
         bars via forward-fill. The regime_window=500 averages ~5 distinct
         prediction values (500/96=5.2). This is less granular than the old
         broadcast, but still meaningful -- it asks "has the WM been
         consistently bullish over the last ~500 bars?" which 5 sequential
         predictions can answer. In live trading, predictions update per-bar,
         so live will actually have BETTER granularity than the backtest.

  [NOTE] bars_per_day computed from test segment (line 200)
         bpd = n_test / max(test_days, 1). This adapts strategy parameters
         (rebal frequency) to the test segment's volume profile. In live
         trading, bpd is computed from the buffer. Minor mismatch possible
         if OOS volume profile differs from live. Acceptable.

The evaluation pipeline is now free of the three original compounding biases:
  1. Lookahead bias:   ELIMINATED (causal gen_preds)
  2. Short P&L:        N/A (was never in universe_eval/walkforward)
  3. Fee inflation:    N/A (was never in universe_eval/walkforward)
  4. Holdout leakage:  ELIMINATED (OOS only, unseen reserved)

Strategies that pass the corrected walk-forward gates have genuine
out-of-sample alpha after real spot trading costs. If a strategy shows
Sharpe > 0 and beats buy-and-hold in 50%+ of rolling windows across 5+
assets, that IS evidence of profitability.

WHAT THE RESULTS WILL TELL YOU:

  - Strategies that STILL pass after removing lookahead are real.
    Expect fewer strategies to pass. Those that do are higher confidence.

  - Sharpe numbers will be LOWER than previous (biased) runs.
    The magnitude of the drop tells you how much the old results
    were inflated by lookahead.

  - If ZERO strategies pass: the WM signal is too weak to trade after
    costs at the current IC level. This is useful information -- it means
    you need to improve the WM before deploying capital.

  - If some strategies pass: compare the winners to the current deployment
    config. If different strategies rank highest, update signal_engine.py.

WHAT THE RESULTS WILL NOT TELL YOU:

  - Future profitability is not guaranteed by OOS profitability. The OOS
    segment is one historical period. Markets change. Walk-forward across
    multiple rolling windows helps, but does not eliminate regime risk.

  - The unseen segment (90-100%) should be touched ONCE, after you lock
    the final strategy selection, as a last pass/fail sanity check.
    Do NOT iterate on unseen results.
