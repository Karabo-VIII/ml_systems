## The apparatus (what "candidate" means here)
For each TI type: build every config's daily net 2020-2022 -> walk-forward ROLLING-FROM-BAND (each rebalance, pick
the recent-best config among the trailing-positive band; the user's "rolling knowledge + rolling performance") ->
the deployable candidate is that rolling book, judged ALL-WEATHER. The discriminating bar is NOT "preserves vs
buy-hold" (every de-risked TI passes that -- it just confirms beta); it is **TIER-A = participates BOTH bulls AND
is flat-or-positive (>= -5%) in the 2022 BEAR** -- the long-only all-weather grail.

## Hypotheses tested (no shortcuts)
- **Working band + rolling-from-band translates** [SUPPORTED]: the rolling-pick beats the in-sample-peeked static-#1
  in the unseen 2022 bear for most types (the static all-time best overfits the bull). Deploy the rolling band, not
  the #1.
- **Chop -> faster MAs** [REFUTED for a bear-chop]: an efficiency-ratio chop-gate that switches to faster MAs in
  chop made 2022 WORSE for most MA types (SMA -61 vs -32, HMA -51 vs -38) -- in a *downward* chop, faster MAs catch
  more falling-knife entries, not cleaner oscillations. The idea may hold in a *range-bound* chop; it does not in a
  bear. Plain rolling-pick wins.
- **The band clusters across types** [SUPPORTED]: every family produces the same participate-bull/preserve-bear
  shape; the all-weather candidates cluster in trend/momentum/breakout/volume; mean-reversion is the
  preserve-but-don't-participate corner.
